Meet the Team


Aircraft design, as with any other complex system, involves a wide array of engineering, science, and project management skills, making the project inherently multidisciplinary in nature. To cope with these technical requirements, RUM-Air is composed of 45 college students from diverse engineering and business/accounting majors. In total, 31 students participated in the RUM-Air last year, and more than 25% of them received internship or co-op offers after their first semester.


Innovation cannot be achieved without diversity of thought. RUM-Air is made up of a culturally and socially diverse group of members who vary wildly in major, gender, and age. The differences between our members' backgrounds allow for the development of new, unique ideas and challenging technical discussions.


At RUM-Air, each member has the opportunity to develop the technical and communication skills to be successful in today's interdisciplinary work environment. To achieve this, team organization consists of two parallel structures: aircraft components and departments. Most members are assigned to the design and manufacture of one aircraft component while supporting the entire project with their work at a specific department. Project Management department members are distributed between aircraft to provide an effective interface between planning and executing the team's strategy. Each aircraft and department has a leader who coordinates their group's efforts. This organization provides a holistic approach that effectively promotes synergy among team members and develops their engineering, critical thinking, and collaboration skills in one semester.
